CFPB data shows that Navient gets the most complaints over summer break.



Navient Headquarters in Wilmington, DE | Delaware Business Times



by Cooper Fojas - Apr. 20, 2023

Navient's highest rate of complaints occurs in the summer months while school is out, according to data from the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau. A Student Loan Planner article from February detailed a $1.85 billion settlement ordered by 39 state attorneys general. The settlement came after complaints against the student loan conglomerate had been piling up since the mid 2010's. Now, CFPB's data shows where and when the complaints seem to happen the most.


The data shows that while October is the month with the highest rate of complaints overall, the period from May to August is actually the most consistently high rate. The data is also split up by region, highlighting complaints from the New York Tri-state area (NYC), the Washington metro area (DMV), and the state of California. Also of note is that the lowest two months are September and February, the two months that kick off the Fall and Spring semesters, respectively.

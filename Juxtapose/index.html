Looking back on the illustrious 22-year career of Albert Pujols



written by Cooper Fojas - Apr. 24, 2023

Albert Pujols's MLB journey began when he was selected 402nd overall in the 1999 draft by the St. Louis Cardinals. Almost 24 years later, Pujols's recent retirement marks the end of a hall-of-fame career that spanned over two decades. The Dominican slugger hung his cleats up after winning two World Series Championships with the Cardinals, three National League MVP awards and 11 All-Star selections. Pujols burst onto the scene in the early 2000s and quickly made his mark as one of the most feared hitters in all of baseball. Injuries, age and the shift had a significant effect on his career by the mid-2010s, but Pujols had a late resurrection, hitting his 700th homerun in his final season.

In total, Pujols spent 12 seasons with the Cardinals, 9 1/2 seasons with the Angels, and half a season with the Dodgers. Some of his additional accolades are listed below:

  • 2 Gold Glove Awards (2006, 2010)
  • 6 Silver Slugger Awards (2001, 2003, 2004, 2008-10)
  • 2 NL Hank Aaron Awards (2003, 2009)
  • NL Comeback Player of the Year (2022)
  • 2nd in Runs Batted In, Career (2218)
  • 4th in Home Runs, Career (703)
  • 5th in Games Played, Career (3080)
